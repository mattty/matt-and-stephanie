Our Story

First met: 2nd October 2005
Engaged: 14th May 2014
Married: 26th April 2015

2005: Stephanie and Matt meet at the London School of Economics, where Stephanie is studying for a year abroad and Matt is in his final year along with his groomsman, Simon. Stephanie and Matt first meet while he is representing the LSE Music Society at Fresher’s Fair - Stephanie joins the mailing list and receives a non-official Music Society email a few days later.

2006: Stephanie returns to Chicago for her final year of college; Matt joins for three months over the summer. Hyde Park adventures are had, along with Stephanie’s then roommate and bridesmaid, Maomao.

2007: Stephanie moves to New York City to begin working for Standard Chartered Bank. Matt begins working at eBay in London. Much money is spent on air travel.

2008: Matt moves to Skype in London, Stephanie remains at Standard Chartered in New York. More money is spent on air travel.

2009: Stephanie moves to London, having accepted an offer for an internal transfer. She lives in Shoreditch with her best friend and matron of honour, Becky, who is spending the year studying at the London College of Fashion.

2010: Stephanie and Matt enjoy the novelty of living in the same city, like a normal couple.

2011: Matt leaves for Harvard Business School in Boston, while Stephanie stays in London. Matt meets Andrew at HBS, eventual San Francisco neighbor and his best man. Transatlantic travel resumes.

2012: Matt interns at IFTTT in San Francisco; Stephanie visits for a couple of weeks to see what living in SF might be like, never having been west of Austin, TX.

2013: Matt finishes business school and Stephanie leaves her job in London. They arrive in San Francisco within a day of each other. Their boxes from London arrive three months later, having enjoyed the scenic route through the Panama Canal.

2014: Matt asks Stephanie to marry him – she says yes.

26 April 2015: PARTY TIME.

Travel

Getting There

Cripps Barn is located in Bibury - a small village in the Cotswolds - an hour and a half northwest of London.

For international guests, we recommend flying into London Heathrow (LHR) and renting a car to travel to Bibury. The full address for the venue is Cripps Barn, Fosscross Lane, Near Bibury, Gloucestershire, GL7 5BA. The GPS co-ordinates for Sat Nav are N51° 46.039’ W1° 52.573’.

Alternatively, you can take a coach to Cirencester (National Express; the 444 to Hereford), which would be a 15 minute taxi ride to Cripps Barn. Or, take a train to Swindon (National Rail), which would then be a 40 minute taxi ride to Cripps Barn.

Getting Around

There is plenty of parking at Cripps Barn and you are welcome to leave cars parked overnight until 10am the following morning, as long as they are parked away from the buildings to avoid obstructing early morning deliveries.

There will be a taxi service on standby the evening of the wedding, and additional local firms can be found here.

Accommodation

There is a variety of accommodation options nearby, most within just three miles of Cripps Barn. Here are some we recommend:

  • The New Inn - a lovely pub 5 miles from Cripps Barn and run by the same folk. 15 rooms from £60 to £190 per night. Quote our names and wedding date for a 10% discount.
  • Bibury Court - a stunning country house hotel 2 miles from Cripps Barn. Room rates range from £125 to £345.
  • Cirencester Travelodge - nearby budget option. Double rooms start at £29 if you book in advance.
  • Feeling adventurous? You can camp onsite at Cripps Barn! Bring your own tent, or have someone do it for you
  • There are also a number of delightful B&Bs in the area as well as self-catering cottages that would be well-suited for groups and families. Click here for a list of options.

Things to do

Bibury is a lovely part of the Cotswolds, with lush rolling hills and honey-coloured limestone villages. In addition to taking in views of the English countryside, here are a few suggested activities during your time here.

  • Cotswald Farm Park - home to more than 50 rare breeds and a great family destination as children can touch the animals and help feed them.
  • Cirencester Park - a majestic country house exemplifying some of the finest geometric landscaping in the country.
  • The Swan Brasserie - seasonally influenced European-style food alongside fine wines; also a hotel.
